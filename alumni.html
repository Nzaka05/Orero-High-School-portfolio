From Homa Bay to Johns Hopkins

Dr. Aran Lloyd's Journey to Neurosurgery Excellence

Growing up in rural Homa Bay, Dr. Aran Lloyd never imagined he would one day perform life-saving brain surgeries at one of America's most prestigious hospitals. His journey began at Orero High School, where dedicated teachers recognized his potential and nurtured his love for science. "Mrs. Kemunto, my biology teacher, stayed after school every day to help me with extra lessons," recalls Dr. Lloyd. "She saw something in me that I didn't even see in myself." Today, as one of the few African male neurosurgeons in the United States, Dr. Lloyd has performed over 500 successful brain surgeries and established a foundation that provides medical scholarships for girls and boys from rural Kenya.

Powering Africa's Green Future

How James Kiprop Built Kenya's Leading Renewable Energy Company

In 2015, James Kiprop noticed that his grandmother in rural Migori still relied on kerosene lamps for lighting. This observation sparked an idea that would eventually become GreenTech Solutions Kenya, now the country's fastest-growing solar energy company. "At Orero High, we learned that innovation comes from identifying real problems and applying disciplined thinking to solve them," says Kiprop. Starting with a small loan and a deep understanding of rural energy needs, he developed affordable solar solutions specifically designed for rural African communities. Today, GreenTech Solutions has brought clean energy to over 200,000 households across East Africa and employs more than 500 people, with 60% being women from rural communities.

A Voice for Global Youth

Charles Abuor Champions Young People at the United Nations

When Charles Abuor speaks at the United Nations headquarters in Geneva, world leaders listen. As the youngest person ever appointed as UN Youth Ambassador, he represents the voices of over 1.8 billion young people globally. His path to this prestigious role began in the debate club at Orero High School. "Our school taught us that every voice matters, regardless of where you come from," reflects Charles. "That confidence gave me the courage to speak truth to power on the global stage." He has successfully advocated for increased youth representation in climate change negotiations and spearheaded initiatives that have created over 10,000 youth employment opportunities across Africa.
