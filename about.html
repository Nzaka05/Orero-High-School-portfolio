About Orero High School

Since its founding in 1968, Orero High School has been a pillar of academic excellence in Homa Bay County — nurturing disciplined, capable, and purpose-driven leaders for Kenya and beyond.

Aerial View of Orero High School

Our Story

Orero High School was established in Rangwe Constituency, Homa Bay County in 1968. What began as a small rural boys’ school has since transformed into one of Kenya’s most consistent performers in academics and co-curricular achievements.

Through visionary leadership, unwavering discipline, and a culture of hard work, the school has consistently produced top KCSE candidates, university-bound graduates, and nationally recognized alumni.

Our Journey Through Time

Key milestones in our four-decade journey of educational excellence

1968 - Foundation

Orero High School was founded in Rangwe, Homa Bay County with the vision of shaping disciplined, academically excellent young men from across Kenya.

1992 - First KCSE Cohort

The first KCSE class graduates with impressive results, earning praise for discipline, academic strength, and university entry rates.

2000 - Facilities Upgrade

Classrooms, science labs, dormitories, and the administration block expanded to support rapid growth in student population.

2018 - National Spotlight

With a KCSE mean score of 9.41 and 100% university transition, Orero rose to national acclaim as one of Kenya’s best-performing public schools.

2021 - Regional Dominance

Ranked 1st in Nyanza and 14th nationally, Orero solidified its place among Kenya’s elite high schools.

2024 - Record Breaking

665 candidates sat the KCSE, achieving a historic mean score of 9.42 with a 100% university qualification rate. Orero’s legacy continues to grow stronger.
